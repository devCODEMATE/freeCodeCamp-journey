Pasta Frola de Batata

I've been making pasta frola for a long time. And this one, like many recipes, I always made "by feel." Culinary art, like many others, involves a bit of that...learning the technique, a few tricks and details, and then going out to create. This time, we're going for the complete recipe.

Ingredients

  • 100g butter
  • 150g sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • 400g self-raising flour
  • 500g sweet potato

      • Instructions

      1. DOUGH Mix and beat the butter (or margarine) with the sugar. Add the eggs one by one and mix well.
      2. Add 300 grams of flour and mix everything until the ingredients are combined. Turn the dough out onto the work surface on top of the remaining flour.
      3. Knead until you obtain a smooth dough...almost sticky to the touch. It shouldn't be too wet or too hard. Separate 1/4 of the dough.
      4. Line a baking dish with the remaining dough
      5. FILLING. Cut the quince paste into small cubes and place it in a saucepan. Add a small cup of water to the saucepan.
      6. Cook over very low heat until the paste softens. Use a fork and a wooden spatula to mash any “stubborn pieces.”
      7. ASSEMBLY. Spread the filling over the pan already lined with dough. Cut the reserved 1/4 of dough in half.
      8. Use one half to make long dough strips and place them over the filling. Make more strips to layer and crisscross over the first ones.
      9. BAKING. Bake the pasta frola in a low oven for 50 minutes.
